General Principles

The apps are designed to process data locally on the user's device where possible. Several apps handle sensitive user content such as photos, documents, voice notes, calendar information or inventory data. The exact processing depends on the app and the permissions granted by the user in iOS.

iOS Permissions

Apps may request access to iOS features such as Photos, Camera, Microphone, Speech Recognition, Calendar, Files or Notifications. Access is only available after the user grants the corresponding iOS permission. Permissions can be changed later in iOS Settings.

Local Processing

Where an app states that processing is local or private, user content is intended to remain on the device unless the user explicitly exports, shares or stores it through an Apple system service or another third-party service.

App-Specific Services

If an individual app uses additional services such as cloud sync, analytics, crash reporting, subscriptions, AI APIs, backend APIs or third-party SDKs, the corresponding app listing, in-app privacy notice or support information should describe that processing separately. This general page does not replace app-specific privacy disclosures where an app has additional data flows.

Purchases

Paid downloads, subscriptions and in-app purchases are handled by Apple. Apple processes purchase, billing and subscription information under Apple's own terms and privacy policy.

Support

For privacy questions about a specific app, contact hello@hoppedev.app and include the app name.
